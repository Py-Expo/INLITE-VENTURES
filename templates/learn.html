  1. Step 1: Local Anesthesia with Sedation or General Anesthesia

    Depending on your case, you'll receive local anesthesia around the insertion point with sedation or possibly general anesthesia to make you sleep.

  2. Step 2: Catheter Insertion

    A thin, flexible tube called a catheter is inserted into an artery in your groin or arm (femoral or radial access) using a needle and guidewire. X-ray imaging helps guide the catheter to the blocked artery.

  3. Step 3: Balloon Angioplasty

    A deflated balloon on the catheter tip is positioned at the blockage. The balloon is then inflated, widening the narrowed artery.

  4. Step 4: Stent Placement

    A collapsed stent pre-loaded on a delivery balloon is positioned within the narrowed segment.

  5. Step 5: Stent Expansion

    The balloon within the stent inflates, expanding the stent and pushing against the artery wall to hold it open. The balloon is then deflated and removed, leaving the stent in place.

  6. Step 6: Verification and Closure

    X-ray imaging confirms proper stent placement and blood flow. The catheter is removed, and the access point is closed with pressure or a closure device.
